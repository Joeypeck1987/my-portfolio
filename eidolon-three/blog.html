An Evening Dance

May 9, 2026

category: lyric sketch

A fuliginous hush falling like snow

bruised dawn, aeneous decay.

Vespertine heart of memory’s slope,

A lost child leads the way.


Do they speak to me,

Or am I just a ghost in there

A flicker in a broken lamp,

Ash suspended in the air?


Time is dust in an ossuary,

Yet my voice, unbearably young.

The ghosts who shaped me,

A still abderian tongue.


If dreams endured a pernoctation,

Would I rise in that eldritch glow

Or fall unheard into the margins

Where the lost souls go?


Do they speak to me,

Or am I just a ghost in there

A flicker in a broken lamp,

Ash suspended in the air?

Chiaroscuro fingers

May 10, 2026

category: lyric sketch

Pale lamp of the sky, a noctilucent breath,

Brushes my face like velvet damp with rain.

The garden lies in ink, each statue held in stillness,

Marble eyes unblinking, watching what remains.


Chiaroscuro fingers paint across my skin,

Every step a dirge, yet sweet as mandolin.


Life is the darkling hymn,

Drawn to the pulse of dim

Where silence turns to sound,

And every tether loosens underground.


Through arbor and arch I wander, solivagant,

Collecting hours in an hourglass of bone.

The air tastes ferrous, like rain upon iron,

Even silence here is not alone.


Chiaroscuro fingers paint across my skin,

Every step a dirge, yet sweet as mandolin.


Life is the darkling hymn,

Drawn to the pulse of dim

Where silence turns to sound,

And every tether loosens underground.


Ombre skies lean closer, thick with quiet weight,

The heart becomes a censer, perfumed by its fate.

Memento’s tender gravity, it pulls and will not part,

The night has learned the contours of my heart.


Life is the darkling hymn,

Drawn to the pulse of dim

The moon keeps time in stone,

And I have found the night to be my own.
